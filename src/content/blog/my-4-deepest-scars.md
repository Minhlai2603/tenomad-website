Building a tech startup is a path paved with struggles. Here are my 4 deepest scars and the lessons they taught me:

**Scar #1: Translate your vision into a highly detailed requirements document.**
Never start coding based on a simple idea or a few slides. Write a complete Product Requirements Document (PRD). If the developer does not know *exactly* what to build, they will assume, and assumptions lead to costly rework.

**Scar #2: Always be capitalized with sufficient reserves.**
Development always takes longer and costs more than expected. Maintain contingency capital. Run out of money, and you run out of time.

**Scar #3: Fail Fast.**
If a strategy, feature, or business model is not working, don't drag it out. Acknowledge the failure, cut your losses, and pivot immediately.

**Scar #4: Establish strong developer oversight.**
Keep close track of developer tasks. Check code commits, run daily standups, and test builds continuously. Loose management leads to delayed releases and poor quality.

**Conclusion: The Hybrid Offshoring Model**
To succeed on a budget, combine offshore development with onshore/local product managers. You get the benefit of lower offshore developer costs while maintaining clear communication and strict quality control.
